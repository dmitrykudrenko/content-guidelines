# Appendix A5 — Scaling Playbook

> **⚠ DRAFT — illustrative example.** This is an AI-generated draft intended as a possible illustration and a TODO starting point. It is not a ready-to-use instruction. Review, adapt, and validate before adopting.

## Purpose

A practical guide for Stage 03 — Scaling (→ [08-content-evolution.md](08-content-evolution.md)). Answers: "I have a published article — how do I adapt it for other channels and keep it alive?"

Scaling has two directions:
1. **Rewriting** — adapting for different channels
2. **Improvements** — updating with fresh data and cross-references

## Channel Adaptation Map

For each source article, evaluate which channels to adapt to. Not every article fits every channel.

| Channel | Format | Length | What changes | Best for |
|---------|--------|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Blog (original)** | Full article | 1500–3000 words | Source — everything starts here | All content types |
| **LinkedIn post** | Key insight + hook | 150–300 words | Extract 1 provocative takeaway, add personal angle | Thought Leadership, Data insights, Myth Busters |
| **LinkedIn carousel** | Visual slides | 8–12 slides | Restructure as visual framework, 1 idea per slide | Systematizations, How-to steps, Comparisons |
| **Email newsletter** | Teaser + link | 100–200 words | Tease the most surprising finding, link to full article | All — but tailor the hook to subscriber segment |
| **Twitter/X thread** | Numbered points | 5–10 tweets | Break into standalone insights, each tweet works alone | Tips, Data findings, Myth Busters |
| **Instagram** | Visual + caption | 1 image + 150 words | Single visual takeaway, strong graphic, caption tells the story | Infographics, Stats, Before/after |
| **Webinar** | Presentation | 30–45 min | Expand with live demos, Q&A, deeper examples | How-to Guides, Research, Case Studies |
| **Podcast mention** | Talking point | 3–5 min segment | Reference the article, add verbal context and anecdotes | Thought Leadership, Interviews, Trends |
| **Whitepaper** | Extended analysis | 3000–5000 words | Add methodology, full data tables, deeper context | Research, Strategy, Comparisons |
| **Infographic** | Single visual | 1 page | Distill the core framework or data into one visual | Systematizations, Stats, Comparisons |
| **Presentation deck** | Slides | 15–25 slides | Key points + visuals, no wall-of-text slides | Conference talks, Webinars, Internal training |

## Adaptation Checklist

For each channel adaptation, check these:

- [ ] **Core message preserved** — the key insight is the same across all channels
- [ ] **Format is native** — the content feels natural for the platform (not a blog post pasted into LinkedIn)
- [ ] **Standalone value** — the adaptation works without reading the original article
- [ ] **Link back** — includes a link or reference to the full article where relevant
- [ ] **CTA adjusted** — the call to action fits the platform (subscribe on blog, follow on LinkedIn, reply on email)
- [ ] **Visual assets created** — each platform has appropriately sized visuals

## Improvement Cycle

Beyond channel adaptation, Scaling means keeping content alive. Schedule these updates:

### Every 3 months
- [ ] Check if statistics and data are still current
- [ ] Add cross-references to newly published related articles
- [ ] Update product screenshots if UI changed

### Every 6 months
- [ ] Refresh with new data points, case studies, or testimonials
- [ ] Add newly available keywords based on SEO performance
- [ ] Review comments and questions — address common ones in the article

### Every 12 months
- [ ] Full content audit — is the article still relevant?
- [ ] Consider merging with other articles if the topic has evolved
- [ ] Evaluate: is this piece ready to move to Product stage? (→ [A1-content-maturity-map.md](A1-content-maturity-map.md))

## Transformation Example

**Source article:** "Types of personalization in email marketing" (Stage: Article)

| Channel | Adapted content |
|---------|----------------|
| **LinkedIn post** | "Most email marketers think personalization = adding {{first_name}}. But there are 3 levels — and 90% of teams are stuck at Level 1. Here's the framework we use at Stripo to diagnose which level you're at..." [link to full article] |
| **Newsletter teaser** | "Subject: You're probably only doing Level 1 personalization. We mapped email personalization into 3 levels. Which one are you at? → Read the full framework" |
| **Infographic** | A single vertical graphic showing the 3 levels as a pyramid: Level 1 (merge tags) → Level 2 (dynamic blocks) → Level 3 (behavioral) with % of teams at each level |
| **Webinar** | 30-min session: "Beyond {{first_name}}: A live walkthrough of advanced personalization in Stripo" — adds live demo component → moves piece toward Product stage |
| **Twitter thread** | "Email personalization has 3 levels. Most teams are stuck at Level 1. A thread 🧵: Tweet 1: Level 1 — merge tags... Tweet 2: Level 2 — dynamic blocks... Tweet 3: Level 3 — behavioral... Tweet 4: Here's how to diagnose your level... Tweet 5: Full framework on our blog: [link]" |

Notice how each adaptation transforms the content for the platform rather than just shortening it. The LinkedIn post leads with a provocative claim. The newsletter creates curiosity. The infographic is purely visual. The webinar adds product demo.
