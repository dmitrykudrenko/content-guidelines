# Valuable Information — Concept

> Status: `DRAFT` — transferred from original document

Valuable information enhances understanding, solves problems, and aids decision-making. It's presented clearly to maximize usefulness. It cannot be something that ChatGPT generates without unique context — if AI can write it without our experience, it's not valuable content.

## 3 Types of Additional Valuable Information (AVI)

### 01 — Systematization (Problem Analysis)

Break down and organize the problem clearly. This step helps in understanding the issue deeply and sets the stage for finding solutions.

**Example:** Instead of "5 tips for email design" → systematize all approaches to email design into a framework with clear categories and selection criteria.

### 02 — Unique Knowledge (Solution Ideas)

Propose innovative solutions using unique insights. Offer new perspectives that can be tested. Learning comes from both successes and failures.

**Example:** Share results of our A/B tests, internal statistics, insights from working with customers — things no one else can know.

### 03 — Product Mastery (Implementation)

Apply and share how solutions work in practice. Document both what works and what doesn't — this guides others in practical application and learning.

**Example:** Step-by-step implementation of a solution through our product with real results, screenshots, and metrics.

## VI Levels in Content

Each content type requires a different level of Valuable Information:

| VI Level | What it means | Where it's used |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **0** | Basic content without AVI | Support docs, SEO articles, landing pages |
| **1** | At least 1 type of AVI | Guest posts, SEO, marketing |
| **2** | 2 types of AVI, deeper analysis | Industrial/marketing content |
| **3** | All 3 types of AVI, maximum depth | Product content, Big Idea, eBooks |

→ How levels map to content types: [04-content-types-and-formats.md](04-content-types-and-formats.md)

## Publication Order

Strategic order when creating a series of notes on one topic:

1. **Systematization** → define and classify the problem
2. **Unique Knowledge** → think about possible resolutions, test them (it's OK to be mistaken or fail — any work like this is worth publishing)
3. **Product Mastery** → share implementation and practical results

This order is logical: first we break down the problem, then we look for solutions, then we show the implementation.
